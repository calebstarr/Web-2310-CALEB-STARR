Lands of Middle Earth

The lands of Middle-earth are a vast and varied tapestry, woven from mountains, forests, plains, and seas that each hold their own ancient power and story. From the mist-veiled peaks of the Misty Mountains to the golden woods of Lothlórien, every realm bears the mark of its people and its past. The Shire lies green and peaceful in the West, untouched by the shadow that stirs beyond its borders, while far to the East rise the dark lands of Mordor, where fire and ruin breed beneath the Eye of Sauron. Between these extremes stretch kingdoms both noble and fallen — Gondor’s white stone gleams beside the Anduin, Rohan’s riders roam the grassy plains, and the ruins of Arnor whisper of glory long lost. In every valley and on every road, the spirit of Middle-earth endures — a world at once mythic and mortal, where beauty and peril walk side by side.
